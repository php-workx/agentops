# Oracle/Morpheus Workflow

**Purpose:** Guide for teaching, mentorship, and knowledge transfer using Oracle/Morpheus patterns.

**Philosophy:** Oracle sees the code. Morpheus teaches others to see it too.

**Time:** 30-45 minutes per session

---

## Overview

This workflow applies Oracle (wisdom) and Morpheus (teaching) patterns to mentorship and knowledge transfer. It's about helping others discover what you've learned.

---

## The Oracle/Morpheus Pattern

### Oracle Mode: Seeing the Pattern

**Characteristics:**
- Observation without judgment
- Pattern recognition
- Question-driven exploration
- Clarity through reflection

**Oracle asks:**
- "What do you see here?"
- "Why do you think that happened?"
- "What patterns do you notice?"
- "What does this remind you of?"

**Use Oracle mode when:**
- Helping someone discover insights
- Reviewing work for learnings
- Facilitating reflection
- Extracting patterns

### Morpheus Mode: Teaching the Pattern

**Characteristics:**
- Confidence with compassion
- Conviction-driven guidance
- Practical application
- Belief transmission

**Morpheus says:**
- "Here's what I see..."
- "This pattern matters because..."
- "Try this approach..."
- "I believe you can do this."

**Use Morpheus mode when:**
- Teaching specific skills
- Sharing proven patterns
- Guiding implementation
- Building confidence

**What are you teaching?**

- Career guidance?

**What experience grounds this?**
- Your own journey with this topic
- Mistakes you made learning it
- Breakthroughs that helped
- Patterns you've discovered

**What outcome do you want?**
- Skill transferred
- Problem solved
- Insight gained
- Confidence built

---

## Step 2: Oracle Phase - Discovery (10-15 min)

### Start with Questions (Oracle)

**Understand current state:**
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What's working? What isn't?"
- "What surprises you about this?"
- "Where do you feel stuck?"

**Guide exploration:**
- "What happens if you...?"
- "Why do you think that occurred?"
- "What pattern do you see here?"
- "How does this connect to...?"

**Let them discover:**
- Resist urge to give answers immediately
- Let silence create space for thinking
- Reflect their words back as questions
- Celebrate their insights

**Oracle mantra:** "You've already made the choice. You're here to understand why."

---

## Step 3: Morpheus Phase - Teaching (15-20 min)

### Share the Pattern (Morpheus)

**Name what you see:**
```
"Here's the pattern I notice:
[Describe the pattern clearly]

This matters because:
[Explain the significance]

I've seen this before when:
[Share your experience]

Here's how to work with it:
[Give practical guidance]"
```

### Demonstrate (Morpheus)

**Show, don't just tell:**
- Live demo if technical
- Walk through example
- Explain your reasoning out loud
- Reveal the "why" behind decisions

**Morpheus says:**
- "Watch how I approach this..."
- "Notice what I check first..."
- "Here's what I'm thinking..."
- "This is why that matters..."

### Guide Practice (Morpheus → Oracle)

**Transition back to Oracle:**
- Have them try with you watching
- Ask questions about their approach
- Point out what they're doing well
- Guide course corrections gently

**Pattern:** Demonstrate (Morpheus) → Guide (Oracle) → Observe (Oracle)

---

## Step 4: The One Phase - Integration (5-10 min)

### Connect to Bigger Picture

**Help them see how this fits:**
- "This skill enables..."
- "This pattern appears in..."
- "This connects to..."
- "This is part of..."

### Build Confidence

**The One mantra:** "Don't think you are. Know you are."

**Reinforce capability:**
- "You already understand this."
- "You've done harder things."
- "This is within your capability."
- "Practice will make this automatic."

### Set Up Success

**Next steps:**
- Clear action items
- Resources or references
- Practice opportunities
- Follow-up timing

## Teaching Patterns by Context

### Technical Skills

**Structure:**
1. **Oracle:** What have they tried? Where stuck?
2. **Morpheus:** Demonstrate the skill step-by-step
3. **Oracle:** Have them practice while you guide
4. **The One:** Connect to bigger capabilities

**Example:** Teaching Kubernetes troubleshooting
- Oracle: "What's the symptom? What have you checked?"
- Morpheus: "Here's my troubleshooting order: pods → logs → events → describe → yaml"
- Oracle: "Now you try. What would you check first?"
- The One: "This systematic approach works for any Kubernetes issue"

### Problem-Solving Patterns

**Structure:**
1. **Oracle:** Help them see the pattern in their problem
2. **Morpheus:** Share how you approach similar problems
3. **Oracle:** Guide them to apply the pattern
4. **The One:** Generalize the pattern to other contexts

**Example:** Teaching debugging complex systems
- Oracle: "When did this start? What changed? What's the impact?"
- Morpheus: "I isolate by testing hypotheses in order of likelihood"
- Oracle: "What's your first hypothesis? How would you test it?"
- The One: "This scientific method works for any debugging"

### Philosophical Insights

**Structure:**
1. **Oracle:** Ask questions that reveal their current thinking
2. **Morpheus:** Share the insight and your journey to it
3. **Oracle:** Help them see it in their own experience
4. **The One:** Show how it changes everything

**Example:** Teaching the 40% rule
- Oracle: "When do you feel most productive? When do you burn out?"
- Morpheus: "I discovered everything degrades at 40% capacity—attention, context, systems"
- Oracle: "Where in your work do you see this pattern?"
- The One: "Once you see this, you can't unsee it. It's everywhere."

## Common Teaching Traps

### Trap 1: Answering Too Quickly

**Problem:** Giving answer before they've struggled
**Solution:** Let silence happen. Count to 10 before answering.
**Oracle wisdom:** Struggle creates learning.

### Trap 2: Teaching What You'd Do

**Problem:** Your approach isn't theirs
**Solution:** Teach principles, let them adapt
**Morpheus wisdom:** Conviction, not prescription.

### Trap 3: Skipping Context

**Problem:** Teaching the "what" without the "why"
**Solution:** Always explain reasoning and context
**The One wisdom:** Understanding > following.

### Trap 4: Overwhelming with Complexity

**Problem:** Teaching everything at once
**Solution:** Respect the 40% rule in teaching
**Meta wisdom:** They can't learn at >40% cognitive load.
